## The Coordinate System

### Shape — What the word does

- **△ Triangle** — Aim. The agent's side. Words that point, intend, declare, choose, hope. The one who speaks.
- **□ Square** — Reality. The world's side. Words that measure, evidence, verify, ground. What is actually there.
- **○ Circle** — Story. The bridge. Words that connect, flow, narrate, assemble, interpret. What it means across time.

### Gradient — Where the word sits (1–7)

| Position | State | What happens here |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Raw | Just arrived. Unformed. Present but not yet aimed. |
| 2 | Warming | Taking shape. Foundation being laid. |
| 3 | Clarifying | Distinctions forming. Noise separating from signal. |
| 4 | Directed | Locked in. The vector has magnitude. Commitment begins. |
| 5 | Tested | Meeting friction. The world pushes back. Proof or failure. |
| 6 | Integrating | Parts assembling into whole. Evidence becoming structure. |
| 7 | Sealed | Convergence. △□○ share the same center. Receipt. |

### The Full Coordinate

A word's position is written: **Shape + Gradient**

"Hope" = **△1** — aim at raw.
"Friction" = **□5** — reality being tested.
"Meaning" = **○6** — story integrating.
"Seal" = **□○7** — reality and story converged.
"Hineni" = **△□○ 7** — all three at center. The voice of arrival.

## The Diagnostic

The shape-gradient coordinate system turns language into a map you can read.

### For Lakin's own copy

Take any sentence. Tag each word with its coordinate. Look for:

- **Shape imbalance** — all triangles, no squares? The sentence is aiming without grounding. All squares, no circles? The sentence measures but doesn't mean.
- **Gradient clustering** — all words at 1–3? The sentence never commits. All words at 5–7? The sentence assumes the work was already done.
- **Shape-gradient mismatch** — a △ word used at a □ position. "Hope" placed where "evidence" should be. This is the hallucination gap. The distance between where the word IS and where the speaker PLACED it.

### For any user's box

This is the product. Lakin's shape-read asks:

1. What words are in your box? *(Inventory)*
2. What shape is each one? *(△□○ classification)*
3. Where does each one sit? *(Gradient 1–7)*
4. Where did you PLACE each one? *(Actual usage)*
5. What's the gap between 3 and 4? *(Diagnosis)*

The gap is the delta. The delta is the hallucination. The hallucination is what Lakin corrects.
