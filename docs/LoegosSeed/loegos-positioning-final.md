## The Four Shapes

Four types. Both the grammar and the geometry.

### △ Aim

What was intended, promised, or declared. The triangle points. It creates a vector. Without aim, there is nothing to test.

Verbs: declare, name, sharpen.

### □ Reality

What pushed back with evidence, constraint, or measurement. The square has edges. It constrains. A photo, a conversation, a number, a person's feedback. Not interpretation. What you can point at.

Verbs: capture, listen, inspect.

### œ Weld

Where aim and reality meet and return a signal. The ligature joins two things that were separate. Not compromise — honest convergence. Blue from the left (aim), green from the right (reality). When they meet, the œ activates.

Verbs: stage, rewrite, operate.

### 𒐛 Seal

What survived contact and can travel as proof. The cuneiform seven. Not arbitrary — requires preconditions: weld must exist, convergence ≥ 0.7, trust ≥ L2, depth ≥ 3. A type-checked commit. If preflight fails, seal is rejected.

Verbs: review, seal, share.

## Triangulating Meaning

Every word, block, and card carries three channels:

1. **Shape** — what kind of thing it is (△ □ œ 𒐛)
2. **Signal** — what state it's in (green / amber / red)
3. **Position** — where in the seven-stage gradient

Two channels give you a line. Three give you a point. When the channels agree, meaning is fixed. When they disagree, the disagreement IS the diagnostic.

### Mobile: The Reality Sensor

You walk through the world. Reality speaks. You catch it.

- **Capture:** Photo, voice, or text. Pick a shape. One sentence. Into the box.
- **Compass:** Phase distribution. Heavy blue = too many promises. Heavy green = time to weld.
- **Radar:** Open aims ranked by convergence.
- **Stream:** Chronological logbook. Timestamped. Geotagged.

Capture on phone, compile on desktop. Same box. Same language. Different posture.

### Seven: The Inference Agent

Seven infers. Seven does not interpret. Seven does not commit.

Seven parses, flags, compares, links, scores, proposes, preflights. Seven never decides what something means. Seven never judges whether convergence is enough. Seven never seals.

Output reads like a debugger — inference, not judgment:

- "This block is labeled △ but reads like □. Recast?"
- "Convergence at 67%. One unresolved aim."
- "Seal preflight fails: trust floor not met."
- "Shadow type: Block 3 and Block 7 — same claim, different labels."

The human reads Seven's inference and interprets. The human is the interpreter of record. Consent before compute.

## The Formal Core

Not a metaphor for a language. A language.

- **Grammar** — production rules from Program to Word.
- **Types** — four shapes with preconditions. Seal requires weld + convergence + trust + depth.
- **Evaluation** — convergence from aim-reality alignment. Hex from six edge scores. Meaning from triangulation.

## Why This Matters

### The Problem

Every coordination tool treats coordination as vague human activity. The tools record what people said. They do not verify whether promises were kept or evidence supports the claims.

Coordination failure is treated as a human problem. But it is a type problem. Promises without evidence are untyped. Conclusions without convergence are premature seals. Plans without reality checks are aim without ground.

### The Insight

Coordination has computable structure. Promises can be typed. Evidence can be required. Convergence can be measured. Closure can be validated. Rerouting can be first-class.

Treat coordination as a language and coordination failure becomes a detectable error rather than a vague human mess.

### The Product

The user writes coordination programs. The system type-checks. Seven infers. The human interprets and commits. When it compiles, you get a receipt — portable, proven, accountable.

Not a dashboard. An IDE. You author. Seven infers. You interpret. When it compiles, reality signed off. AI structures and infers; humans interpret and commit.

## The Hex

Flat-top hexagon. Six edges. No gradient fill.

Why hexagon: Seven lives in the glyph (𒐛), not the polygon count. Hexagon is honeycomb — cells that fit together, building structure.

Why no fill: the edges carry meaning. Six edges, each with its own signal color. One hex = more information than a full card. Aim completeness, evidence quality, convergence strength, weld validity, depth distribution, seal integrity.

Six edges + one center = seven. Three opposing pairs = three axes = triangulation rendered as geometry.
