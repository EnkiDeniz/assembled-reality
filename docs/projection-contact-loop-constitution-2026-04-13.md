# Projection And Contact Loop Constitution

Date: April 13, 2026  
Status: Product constitution  
Purpose: Give engineering, design, product, and future collaborators a clean mental model for what Lœgos is fundamentally trying to do, so the runtime can be rebuilt from a truthful center and the eventual user experience can make that center felt without having to explain it.

---

## 1. One Line

**Lœgos is a machine for bringing projection and contact back into relation.**

Or, more operationally:

**The human proposes, the machine structures, reality returns, and receipts decide what can travel.**

This is not marketing copy.
It is the deepest useful compression we have right now for what the product is actually for.

---

## 2. Why This Compression Matters

The current system has accumulated many valid concepts:

- Room
- Dream
- Compiler Read
- Working Echo
- return
- receipts
- visible stabilizer
- Recon
- Drive Tape
- Seven Terminal

These concepts are not equal.
They do not all belong at the same architectural level.

The purpose of this document is to restore the center beneath them.

If we miss that center, the product fragments into:

- chat
- tools
- panels
- traces
- diagnostics
- modes

If we keep the center, those same pieces can be arranged as positions inside one loop.

That loop is:

- projection
- structure
- contact
- return
- travel

This is the architecture the repo has already been moving toward, even when the UI has become noisy.

---

## 3. The Core Distinction

Lœgos is not trying to eliminate projection.
Projection is necessary.

Humans always begin by:

- feeling
- suspecting
- hoping
- naming
- hypothesizing
- projecting a possible read

The problem is not projection.
The problem is when projection quietly impersonates closure.

Likewise, the product is not trying to worship “reality” as a blunt override.
Reality in this architecture is not a magical authority object.
It is what pushes back:

- witness
- contradiction
- consequence
- external condition
- delay
- failure
- surprise
- non-response
- successful return

The product exists because coherence and convergence are not the same thing.
Something can sound complete, beautiful, intelligent, and still not hold.

Lœgos is the instrument that keeps those two apart long enough for an honest next move to appear.

---

## 4. The Product Loop

The system should be understood as one loop with multiple positions inside it, not as multiple separate mini-products.

### 4.1 Propose

The human enters with a read, a question, a fear, a desire, a possible move, or a source.

This is projection in its healthiest sense:

- a draft
- a guess
- an intention
- a provisional structure

The product must make this easy.
Without proposal, there is nothing to test.

### 4.2 Structure

The machine does not author the user’s life.
It structures what is present:

- separates aim from witness
- surfaces contradictions
- marks what is missing
- pressure-tests form
- prevents shape violations from quietly passing as closure

This is where the language and compiler matter.
The machine’s role is not “be smarter than the user.”
Its role is to make the structure of the current read legible and harder to fake.

### 4.3 Contact

The product must then allow actual contact with something outside the projection.

That may be:

- source material
- lived action
- external witness
- bounded test
- waiting for the world to answer
- reading the document again under pressure

This is the step most AI products skip.
They complete the loop internally and call it intelligence.
Lœgos becomes real only if it preserves the gap where the world can answer.

### 4.4 Return

Return is the answer that comes back.

Return may:

- support the read
- weaken it
- contradict it
- split it
- force a rename
- reopen what was prematurely closing

The product should treat return as first-class.
Not a comment after the fact.
Not metadata.
Not a nuisance.

Return is what prevents the machine from becoming a self-sealing story engine.

### 4.5 Travel

Not everything that appears in the loop should travel forward.
Only what has survived sufficient pressure should be allowed to carry weight into the future.

That is what receipts are for.

A receipt is not merely a log.
It is a portability decision:

- what can travel
- at what trust
- with what evidence chain
- under what caveats

This is why the sentence matters:

**The human proposes, the machine structures, reality returns, and receipts decide what can travel.**

---

## 5. Product Positions, Not Product Modes

This is the critical architectural consequence.

We should not rush to turn each position in the loop into a separate visible mode.
The user should feel one living instrument, not a family of disconnected tools.

The internal positions are something like:

- `Listen`
- `Tell`
- `Work`
- `Sense`
- `Seal`

These are not necessarily route names.
They are functional positions inside the loop.

**These are loop positions, not UI destinations by default.**
They should not quietly become five future tabs, routes, or equal-weight shell sections just because they form a clean conceptual set.

### 5.1 Listen

Absorptive re-entry.
Source contact.
Reading.
Listening.
Letting the material shape the next proposal.

This is the deepest truth inside Dream.

### 5.2 Tell

The user’s unstructured or lightly structured proposal.
What they think is happening.
What they think they should do.
What they want to test.

This is the raw energy chat is good at generating.

### 5.3 Work

The crossing where proposal meets limit.
This is where structure matters.
This is where the compiler, the working echo, the pressure instruments, and the machine’s assistance matter.

This is the center of Room.

### 5.4 Sense

This is not sensing in the UI-tooltips sense.
It is sensing in the “what came back?” sense.

Reality has answered somehow:

- a source did not support the story
- a move failed
- a contradiction appeared
- the witness changed
- the market shifted
- the person answered

This is return.

### 5.5 Seal

Some part of the loop can now travel.
Not because it is perfect, but because it has enough pressure behind it to carry responsibly.

That is receipt logic.

Again: these are positions in one loop.
Not necessarily five buttons.

## 6. Mapping This To The Product

### 6.1 Room

Room is where proposal and contact are actively braided in live time.
Room is not just chat.
**Room becomes Lœgos through the working echo.**

The thread is where the conversation moves.
The working echo is where the live structural read becomes visible enough to steer by.
Without the working echo, Room can collapse back into a well-designed assistant chat surface.
With it, Room becomes a steering instrument.

Room should therefore feel like:

- the place where the live question is worked
- the place where the next move becomes clearer
- the place where return can bend the read

Room should not feel like:

- a dashboard
- a project operating system
- a theory exposition layer
- a panel farm

The center of Room is the live crossing.

### 6.2 Dream

Dream is not merely “the library.”
Dream is the source re-entry surface.

Dream should feel like:

- inward return to source
- re-absorption
- listening
- reading
- slower refinement
- the place where serious documents can be pressure-tested without becoming canon

Dream should not feel like:

- a file manager
- a utility page
- a settings area
- a dead repository of things the user uploaded once and forgot

The center of Dream is contact through source.

### 6.3 Bridge

Bridge is how source contact becomes live steering.

Dream and Room should not become two elegant islands.
The product only stays whole if a user can carry something discovered in source contact into live work without friction and without ontological confusion.

Bridge should therefore mean:

- passage to Room
- witness to Room
- note to Room
- anchor back to Dream when the Room object came from a source

Bridge is not a third permanent mode.
It is the handoff logic that keeps the loop continuous.

### 6.4 Compiler Read

Compiler Read is a pressure instrument inside Dream.

It should feel like:

- serious
- read-only
- clarifying
- humbling in a useful way

It should not feel like:

- automatic proof
- shadow canon
- a second Room
- a second author

Compiler Read belongs in Dream because it is a disciplined way of re-entering serious source material under structure pressure.

### 6.5 Receipts

Receipts are not a mode.
They are the portability law of the system.

They should matter deeply in the backend and in the trust spine even when they are visually quiet.

### 6.6 Recon

Recon is not the current center.
It is a future extension of the same loop across a longer trail.

Base Lœgos asks:

- what is true here?
- what should I do next?

Recon would ask:

- what invisible rule keeps recreating this situation?

That is a second timescale, not a second product.

### 6.7 Drive Tape / Visible Stabilizer

These are legibility instruments.
They should eventually help the user see what held, what drifted, what was rerouted, and what survived.

They are not the main runtime center.
They are observer surfaces for a more mature phase.

### 6.8 Runtime Translation

For implementation planning, the abstract loop should translate into runtime nouns like this:

| Loop role | Runtime noun | Current job |
|---|---|---|
| live crossing | `Room` | hold the thread, composer, and working echo |
| source re-entry | `Dream` | hold documents, reading, listening, and slower refinement |
| handoff | `Bridge` | move passages, witnesses, and notes between Dream and Room |
| live structural surface | `workingEcho` | make the evolving read steerable inside Room |
| source pressure instrument | `CompilerRead` | pressure-test Dream documents without becoming canon |
| portability law | `Receipt` | decide what can travel forward and at what trust |

This translation is intentionally plain.
It keeps the constitution usable for engineering without reducing it to implementation trivia.

## 7. What The User Should Feel

This section is the most important for design and engineering.

The user does not need to be taught the philosophy explicitly.
They should feel it through the product.

### 7.1 They should feel that they can enter unfinished

The product should welcome provisional thought.
It should not require the user to become formal before they begin.

### 7.2 They should feel that eloquence is not enough

The machine should not reward polished story alone.
Something should remain quietly unresolved until witness, return, or pressure justifies more confidence.

### 7.3 They should feel that the world still matters

The product should make it natural to leave and come back.
If a user can “finish” everything inside the app, the architecture has drifted.

### 7.4 They should feel that return changes the read

When new witness arrives, the system should visibly allow it to bend the current structure.
Not merely append to it.

### 7.5 They should feel that not everything deserves to travel

Some thoughts are useful but provisional.
Some structures are real enough to carry.
That difference should be palpable, even if the user never reads a trust policy.

## 8. What Engineers Should Build For

### 8.1 Backend engineers

The backend should preserve the loop honestly.

That means:

- proposal state must remain provisional by default
- return must be first-class, not decorative
- receipts must carry evidence and trust, not just text
- Compiler Read must remain read-only and non-canonical
- Dream and Room must be bridgeable without collapsing into one datastore blur
- future Recon logic should extend the language and compiler, not become parallel machinery

### 8.2 Frontend engineers

The frontend should make the loop felt without teaching it explicitly.

That means:

- Room must open directly into live work
- Dream must privilege document contact over controls
- structural pressure should appear at the right moment, not as ambient clutter
- future features should enter through contextual forms before permanent ones
- the UI must stop explaining the machine and instead let the machine’s behavior teach the user

### 8.3 Everyone

When deciding whether to build or keep something, ask:

1. does this help the user propose?
2. does this help the machine structure?
3. does this improve contact?
4. does this help return actually bend the read?
5. does this help receipts decide what can travel?

If the answer is no, the thing may be interesting but it is not foundational.

---

## 9. Anti-Patterns

These are the failure modes this constitution is trying to prevent.

### 9.1 AI becomes the protagonist

If the user feels they are watching the system think instead of using an instrument to think and test, the center is lost.

### 9.2 Chat becomes closure theater

If chat alone can simulate completion, the system drifts back into projection-only operation.

### 9.3 Dream becomes a utility

If Dream is treated as file storage instead of source re-entry, it will never become a real mode.

### 9.4 Compiler Read becomes shadow canon

If Compiler Read writes back or silently upgrades trust, it breaks the loop.

### 9.5 Return becomes commentary

If the world’s answer is visible but does not structurally matter, the system becomes decorative.

### 9.6 Receipts become logs

If receipts record everything equally instead of deciding what can travel, they lose their function.

### 9.7 Every good idea becomes a route

If each concept earns a top-level surface too early, the runtime will fragment before the foundation is stable.

---

## 10. Design Consequences

This model has a direct effect on interface decisions.

### 10.1 Room should stay simple

Because Room is the live crossing, it should not be crowded with ontology, panels, or theory furniture.

### 10.2 Dream should feel immersive

Because Dream is contact through source, the document should dominate.

### 10.3 Structural objects should be earned

Cards, pulse, diagnostics, and receipts should appear when the loop earns them, not as default decoration.

### 10.4 Modes should be few

Because this is one loop with positions inside it, the visible architecture should remain minimal.

### 10.5 The product should be easy to leave

Because contact with reality is essential, the product must not be optimized for trapping the user inside itself.

---

## 11. Build Rule

The next build rule should be:

> rebuild the runtime so users can feel proposal, structure, contact, return, and travel without having to learn those words

That means:

- keep Room and Dream as the permanent surfaces
- keep Compiler Read inside Dream
- keep receipts and trust real in the backend
- let future capabilities prove themselves before they become runtime architecture

## 13. Article 0 Annex: Load-Bearing Invariants

This constitution should not stand alone.
It is the governing geometry, not the whole car.

It tells us what the product is for and how force should move through it.
It does **not** replace the architecture, the language, or the runtime shape grammar that make this specifically Lœgos.

If this document is Article 0, then this annex protects the things that remain load-bearing even as routes, surfaces, and prototypes change.

### 13.1 The machine structures through a compiler in the middle

When this constitution says **the machine structures**, that must not collapse into generic AI assistance.

In Lœgos, the machine structures through:

- a typed coordination language
- a shape-checking compiler
- a trust spine
- an explicit boundary between human proposal and model output

The rear-engine architectural bet is that the compiler sits between human and model strongly enough to keep projection from quietly hardening into closure.

That is not optional flavor.
It is one of the things that makes this product Lœgos instead of “chat plus analysis.”

### 13.2 The four forces

For runtime thinking, it is useful to treat the main force flow as four active forces:

1. `projection`
   what is proposed, imagined, feared, hoped, or inferred
2. `structure`
   what the language and compiler can separate, type, pressure-test, and hold provisionally
3. `contact`
   what pushes back from outside the current projection
4. `return`
   what comes back after contact and alters the read

`travel` is not a fifth active force in the same sense.
It is the carry-forward law applied after pressure.

Plainly:

**travel = carry forward with trust**

That phrase should always be read epistemically, not as navigation or movement through screens.

### 13.3 Contact has two classes

The constitution intentionally keeps `contact` broad.
For engineering and product work, it helps to separate two contact classes.

#### 13.3.1 Contact-through-source

This is contact by re-entering material:

- rereading
- listening
- close inspection
- source comparison
- document pressure-testing

This is the center of Dream.

#### 13.3.2 Contact-through-world

This is contact by leaving the system and encountering reality directly:

- bounded test
- shipped move
- consequence
- reply from another person
- market response
- failure
- contradiction
- non-response

This is what eventually comes back as return in the live loop.

These two classes are related, but they are not identical.
Treating them as the same event will blur Dream and Room again.

### 13.4 The gap is primary

Lœgos should preserve the gap between:

- what sounds coherent
- what has actually converged through contact

That gap is not a flaw in the product.
It is one of the primary instruments.

If the system closes the gap too fast, it becomes closure theater.
If it makes the gap visible and steerable, it becomes useful.

### 13.5 Crossroads is a real state

The system needs an honest rest state where nothing is falsely resolved and no theatrical move is forced.

That state is the crossroads:

- the loop is open
- no move is yet earned
- return is still awaited
- the user can re-enter honestly

This is one reason `no_move_yet` matters so much.
Crossroads is not failure.
It is the bounded rest state that prevents false motion.

### 13.6 Grace must remain in the machine

This constitution is structurally sharp on purpose.
It must not become cold.

The thermal law is still:

**no shame, only signal**

That means:

- unfinished entries are valid
- correction is not punishment
- reroute is not embarrassment
- provisional thought is not waste
- the bonfire stays warm enough for honest re-entry

Grace should not become a separate reasoning layer.
It should be felt in how the system handles incompleteness, failure, and return.

### 13.7 Seal is a state transition, not a surface

`Seal` belongs in the loop vocabulary, but it should never quietly become a permanent page, route, or destination.

In this constitution, seal means:

- a state transition
- a receipt-qualified carry-forward
- a bounded decision about what can now travel

It does **not** mean:

- total closure
- proof of ultimate truth
- completion theater
- a UI section that deserves permanent navigation weight

## 14. Bridge Table

This table exists so the team can translate constitutional language into runtime decisions without confusing the two levels.

| Constitutional role | Internal loop position | Runtime anchor | What it means now |
|---|---|---|---|
| `propose` | `Tell` | `Room` thread + composer | the human enters with a read, question, move, or source |
| `structure` | `Work` | `workingEcho` + compiler-mediated room behavior | the machine separates, pressures, and makes the live read steerable |
| `contact-through-source` | `Listen` | `Dream` | source re-entry through reading, listening, and document pressure |
| `contact-through-world` | `Sense` precursor | outside the app, then back into `Room` | lived test, consequence, contradiction, or external answer |
| `return` | `Sense` | `Room` via updated echo and thread state | what came back and how it bends the read |
| `travel` | `Seal` | `Receipt` | what may carry forward with trust |

This table should be used as a translation aid.
It should not be mistaken for a mandate to turn each row into a top-level visible surface.
